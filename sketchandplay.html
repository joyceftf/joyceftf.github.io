JO:Sketch&Play

JO:Sketch&Play took place in 2023 within the context of the Paris Olympics and Amiens‘ IC.ON.IC festival, sports and teamship being the very core of these events. EsadType student and designer Manau Quellec managed various workshops relying on the creation of printed numbers out of hand-drawn pictograms to ornate personalized soccer jerseys for each participant to wear.
The printing process has been made possible thanks to designer Thomas Signollet, and type designer Ibrahim Kactioglu also did oversee the workshops at ésad Amiens. The final exhibition took place at the Maison de la Culture of Amiens on November 8th 2023.

Sketch&Play

approach

On your mark, get set, go !


Pictograms were first sketched into squares for uniformity, and based on several categories such as personal information (initials, city of birth, ethnicity...) to hobbies and sports in general. Workshop participants were encouraged to play with letters, shapes and space to generate multiple variations of the same (possible) prompt to assure various graphic outcomes.
The use of large felt pens and black ink eased the sorting drawing and lettering process, simplifying each line drawn onto the paper once scanned and digitalized.
