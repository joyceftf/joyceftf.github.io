Prose and Poetry

  • Editorial

  • Prints


This riso-printed project started from the study of Prose and Poetry, The New Art of Making Books (1975) by Ulises Carrion, and came to light following these few sentences : « Poems are songs, the poets repeat. But they don’t sing them. They write them. Poetry is to be said aloud, they repeat. But they don’t say it aloud. They publish it. The fact is, that poetry, as it occurs normally, is written and printed, not sung and spoken, poetry. And with this, poetry has lost nothing. On the contrary, poetry has gained something: a spatial reality that the so loudly lamented sung and spoken poetries lacked. ».
This editorial reinterpretation of the text is in French.

approach

The Space


Although this project does not include the entirety of said quote, the choice of unfolding paper, scattered poetry and the importance given to white space and hand-drawn lettering was made purposely in order to back up and highlight Carrion’s statement about written text.
This multi-faceted flyer works both as a visual and tactile experience, with the reader being allowed to discover the poetry in a non-linear way, with each page connected thanks to the exaggerated ponctuation.
